The Story

Born into grief.
Built into love.

My parents fled genocide. Sri Lankan Tamil, carrying a country's worth of grief in their bodies — with no language to put it down. They arrived in London and loved the way survivors love: through safety, achievement, and the silence of everything that couldn't be said. I was born into that. I inherited the anxiety before I had words for it.

I was also born with a hand the world decided was something to stare at. I remember being 14, at Tamil school, preparing a speech. There was a part where I was supposed to make a flower bloom with both hands. My mother went quiet. Uncomfortable. Ashamed. I had to do it with one hand. I took her shame and made it mine. I hid that hand for years — at cricket, at dinner tables, in meetings. Every time I hid it, I was agreeing with the world's verdict.

"It is not actually deformed. It is exactly the way God wanted it to be. It's beautiful."

At 24, I hit the floor. I was suicidal, alone, and I made a choice — the only choice that actually matters. I chose to live. And then, bit by bit, I pulled myself out.

At a trauma healing workshop, I stood in front of 200 people and held up the hand that God uniquely crafted for me — but that society had told me was wrong. Something sealed for twenty two years cracked open in that room. That moment was the first act of the work I was born to do.

"Giving up gave me peace. Giving up gave me hope. Giving up helped me connect with God."

I moved to Bali in August 2025. Lost, and not knowing what came next, I asked God what I should do. And God said it — clearly, repeatedly, like a shout: Write the book. Write the book. Write the book. So I did."

The Lion Work A methodology for turning the wound into power
The Method

This work has a name.
It was always yours.

There is a lion tattooed on my hand. The hand I spent years hiding — at dinner tables, in meetings, on dates. Every time I hid it, I was agreeing with the world's verdict about me. The day I stopped hiding it, something shifted that I can't un-shift. That hand is now my most visible symbol of what I know to be true: the wound was always the power.

That's what The Lion Work does. It takes the thing you've been most ashamed of and walks you toward it, not away from it. Three phases. They don't rush. They don't fix. They move through.

"Therapy is a good first step — but a bad place to stay. This work opens the human to infinite possibilities."

01

Pain

Everyone arrives here. Pain clouds everything — judgment, direction, capacity. People are in survival mode and they don't have the space in their hearts for anything else. The work begins by going into the pain rather than around it. Sitting with it. Becoming intimate with it. This is not wallowing — it is the only door that actually opens.

02

Purpose

When someone creates an opening through their pain — when they alchemise it into love — they have more space in their heart to love people. And when they start loving people, that is their purpose. Purpose is not found. It is revealed when the pain clears. It is the way God wants you to love humanity through you.

03

Shine

Everywhere I go, I just shine. I don't know how else to say it. I love myself. People feel that love. I know I'm doing something amazing. I'm close to God. I feel amazing. This is what becomes available on the other side of the work — not a concept of wellbeing, but an actual state of being fully alive in the world.
