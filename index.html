The Story

Born into grief.
Built into love.

My parents fled genocide. Sri Lankan Tamil, carrying a country's worth of grief in their bodies — with no language to put it down. They arrived in London and loved the way survivors love: through safety, achievement, and the silence of everything that couldn't be said. I was born into that. I inherited the anxiety before I had words for it.

I was also born with a hand the world decided was something to stare at. I hid it for years — at cricket, at dinner tables, in meetings. Every time I hid it, I was agreeing with the world's verdict.

"It is not actually deformed. It is exactly the way God wanted it to be. It's beautiful."

At 24, I hit the floor. I was suicidal, alone, and I made a choice — the only choice that actually matters. I chose to live. And then, bit by bit, I pulled myself out.

In Berlin, I stood in front of 200 people and raised my hand. I told them the truth about it. Something sealed for thirty years cracked open in that room. That moment was the first act of the work I was born to do.

"Giving up gave me peace. Giving up gave me hope. Giving up helped me connect with God."

I wrote a book in five days in Ubud. I walked into Sri Lanka and felt home in my DNA for the first time. I am a spiritual healer. That's what felt right. That is what this is.

The Lion Work A methodology for turning the wound into power
The Method

This work has a name.
It was always yours.

There is a lion tattooed on my hand. The hand I spent years hiding — at dinner tables, in meetings, on dates. Every time I hid it, I was agreeing with the world's verdict about me. The day I stopped hiding it, something shifted that I can't un-shift. That hand is now my most visible symbol of what I know to be true: the wound was always the power.

That's what The Lion Work does. It takes the thing you've been most ashamed of and walks you toward it, not away from it. There are four phases. They don't rush. They don't fix. They move through.

"If the whole world is gonna think you're weird — what are you gonna do about it? I chose to fucking love myself so much."

01

Name the Pain

Radical honesty about where you actually are. Not the version you tell people. The thing underneath that. The wound beneath the wound. You cannot move through what you won't face — and most people are running so fast they haven't stopped to look.

02

Go Into It

Not around it. Not above it. Through it. This is where unconditional love enters — not the fixing kind, not the improving kind. The sitting-with kind. The staying kind. The kind that doesn't flinch.

03

Find the Fuel

The shame becomes the offering. The wound was always the material. This is the alchemy — where what broke you becomes what makes you. It doesn't disappear. It converts. It was all energy. It was all potential.

04

Arrive in Love

A self you don't need to perform. A life that feels like yours. Not a destination you reach and park at — a practice you live. Self-love not as a concept but as something you do, every day, until it becomes who you are.
